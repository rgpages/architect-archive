Deconstructing the lawsuit

The climate youth lawsuit claims that the U.S. government has known about the harmful effects of carbon dioxide pollution and that it has not done enough to stop such pollution from causing "catastrophic climate change." The suit includes the following six instances, going back more than 50 years, when the government was presented with this information.

1965

Johnson administration report

President Johnson's scientific advisors issue report stating carbon dioxide and other man-made pollutants threaten the "health, longevity, livelihood, recreation, cleanliness and happiness of citizens who have no direct stake in their production, but cannot escape their influence."

1978

National Climate Program Act

Congress passes National Climate Program Act to "assist the nation and the world to understand and respond to natural and man-induced climate processes and their implications."

Read the legislation here.

1988

Hansen testimony

Climate scientist James Hansen, then-director of NASA's Institute for Space Studies, testifies before Congress that carbon pollution in the atmosphere was causing global warming and that impacts already were being observed.

Read the report here.

1992

U.N. convention

After two federal agencies issue reports calling for reductions in U.S. carbon dioxide emissions, the Senate ratified the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change, which was designed to "protect the climate system for the benefit of present and future generations of humankind."

Read the report here.

2009

EPA endangerment finding

The Environmental Protection Agency issues a formal "endangerment finding" under the Clean Air Act, stating that current and projected concentrations of greenhouse gases threaten the health of current and future generations.

Read the report here.

2015

Climate youth lawsuit

Youth plaintiffs, along with Hansen, sue federal government in U.S. District Court in Eugene, alleging government has created a national energy system that causes climate change and deprives them of their constitutional rights to life, liberty and property.
