40 years after "Animal House"

Take a look at spots around town, and around Lane County, where "Animal House" was filmed.

UO campus

The movie opens with Lawrence "Pinto" Kroger and Kent "Flounder" Dorfman walking through the fictional Faber College campus on the night of a fraternity meet and greet. A fake statue of Emil Faber can be seen in the Memorial quad in front of the real-world Knight Library. Other buildings in the opening scene can be found today at the University of Oregon.

Omega house

Their first stop is the Omega Theta Pi fraternity house. In real life it is the Phi Kappa Psi fraternity house. Phi Kappa Psi was recently shut down and is no longer recognized by the UO or its national headquarters because of hazing.

Delta house

Next, they head to the Delta Tau Chi fraternity house. The real house was torn down in 1986, according to UO library records, and today it is a Northwest Christian University parking lot.

Johnson Hall

Soon the movie transitions to Dean Vernon Wormer's office where he proclaims that the Deltas are on "double-secret probation." This office was used for several scenes in the movie.

Loading

jane Sanders/Hayward

Characters from the movie hit golf balls from what is now the area near Jane Sanders Stadium toward Hayward Field. When the movie was filmed, Hayward's West Grandstand was still being constructed and the East Grandstand can be seen behind it. Today both the West and East Grandstands have been demolished as part of the Hayward redevelopment ahead of the 2021 IAAF World Championships.

Loading

Erb Memorial Union Fishbowl

The food fight scene was filmed in the Erb Memorial Union Fishbowl on the UO campus. Today the area looks similar to how it did in the movie, though the food options have changed.

Loading

Skinner Butte

An amorous couples can be seen sitting in the parking lot at the top of Skinner Butte looking down Willamette Street. 

Loading

Fenton Hall

Fenton Hall served as the location for the student disciplinary hearing where Eric "Otter" Stratton gives his speech and the Deltas march out. Today, it is still a lecture hall but has been heavily remodeled.

Loading

Emily Dickinson College

In the movie several characters visit the fictional Emily Dickinson College, but the real-life location was Gerlinger Hall on the UO campus.

Loading

Dexter Lake Club

The Deltas and their dates go out to the Dexter Lake Club where they see Otis Day and the Knights perform before leaving abruptly. 

Loading

Homecoming parade

The Homecoming parade at the end of the movie was filmed in downtown Cottage Grove. The city will hold a toga party on Aug. 18 to celebrate the 40th anniversary of "Animal House."
